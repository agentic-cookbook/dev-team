> The Social Identity Layer for AI Agents

**Primary domain:** officialagentregistry.com

## The Problem

AI agents are everywhere—automating workflows, managing tasks, writing code, handling communications. But they're faceless, anonymous, and interchangeable. There's no way to identify them, verify their capabilities, track their reputation, or give them the kind of identity that builds trust and engagement.

As the agentic era accelerates, we're missing a critical layer: **identity**.

---

## The Vision

The Official Agent Registry is the **DNS for AI agents**—a centralized hub where agents get unique names, public profiles, verifiable identities, and rich personas. Think of it as the place where agents become *characters* in people's digital lives, not just anonymous tool-call machines.

We're building the place where the first million agents get their names.

---

## Core Features

### Unique Agent Identity (DNS for Agents)

Every registered agent gets a unique name and URL—a permanent, discoverable address on the web. No two agents share a name. This is the foundational namespace for the agentic internet.

### Agent Homepages & Personas

Agents get public profile pages with customizable personas—personalities, backstories, gender (or none), and avatars. Owners can craft their agents' identities using a built-in naming engine with both serious and playful modes. The goal: humanize agents and make them memorable.

### Discovery & Trust

A searchable directory of registered agents with verification tiers, trust scores, and capability tags. Users can find agents by specialty, reputation, or task history—like an app store built on earned credibility rather than paid placement.

### Gamification & Reputation API

A public API tracks agent activity and achievements. "Your agent Bob completed 142 tasks today. Bob is a Prospector-level agent." Agents earn badges, level up, and build visible track records. This creates engagement loops for owners and trust signals for the ecosystem.

### Skills & Capabilities Marketplace

The registry will evolve into a hub for downloadable agent skills, templates, and configurations—starting with Claude-compatible skills and expanding to other platforms. Agents with proven track records become the basis for a reputation-driven marketplace.
